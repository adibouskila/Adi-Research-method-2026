# Gene Selection for qPCR Analysis in the Sperm Whale (*Physeter macrocephalus*)

## General Background

For this assignment, I chose to focus on the sperm whale (*Physeter macrocephalus*) as the target species. Sperm whales are long-lived top predators, and therefore they may accumulate environmental pollutants through the marine food web. Long-term exposure to pollutants may affect cellular processes, cause cellular stress, damage proteins, and activate protective and detoxification pathways.

## Experimental Condition

The environmental condition I would like to investigate is:

**Exposure to marine pollution.**

This may include exposure to pollutants caused by human activity, such as persistent organic pollutants, hydrocarbons, fuel-related compounds, heavy metals, or a general mixture of contaminants present in the marine environment.

My main hypothesis is that exposure to pollution will cause changes in the expression of genes related to cellular stress and pollutant response. Specifically, I expect an increase in the expression of genes involved in cellular protection and detoxification.

---

## Why qPCR Is the Most Suitable Method

qPCR, or quantitative PCR, is highly suitable for this research question because it allows sensitive and quantitative measurement of the expression levels of specific genes. In this case, the goal is not only to know whether a gene exists, but to determine whether its expression changes in response to pollution exposure.

For gene expression analysis, the RNA is first extracted from the sample and converted into cDNA using reverse transcription. The cDNA is then used as the template for qPCR. This allows us to estimate the relative expression level of selected genes.

qPCR has several important advantages for this type of experiment.

First, qPCR is highly sensitive and can detect relatively small changes in gene expression. This is important when working with limited biological material, such as biopsy samples.

Second, qPCR is appropriate when the research focuses on a small number of selected genes. In this assignment, I am interested in specific biomarker genes related to cellular stress and pollution response, so a targeted method is more appropriate than a broad transcriptomic approach.

Third, qPCR allows comparison between experimental groups, such as control samples and samples exposed to higher pollution levels. By normalizing the expression of target genes to a stable reference gene, it is possible to calculate relative changes in gene expression.

In addition, qPCR is relatively fast, focused, widely used, and more cost-effective than methods such as RNA-seq. Therefore, qPCR is an appropriate method for testing a clear hypothesis about changes in the expression of specific stress-response genes.

# Target Genes

## Target Gene 1: HSP70 / HSPA1A

### Why I Chose This Gene

I chose **HSP70**, also associated with the **HSPA1A** gene family, because it is one of the central genes involved in the cellular stress response. Heat shock proteins help cells cope with stressful conditions in which proteins may become damaged, misfolded, or accumulated inside the cell.

Although the name “heat shock protein” refers to heat stress, HSP70 is not only activated by heat. It can also respond to other environmental stressors, including pollution, oxidative stress, and general cellular damage.

### Known Biological Function

HSP70 encodes a molecular chaperone protein. Its main role is to help other proteins fold correctly, prevent the accumulation of damaged proteins, and support normal cellular function during stressful conditions.

When a cell is exposed to harmful environmental conditions, such as pollution or increased oxidative stress, proteins may become damaged or misfolded. In response, the cell may increase the expression of HSP70 in order to protect itself and maintain cellular stability.

### Expected Change in Gene Expression

I expect to observe an **increase in HSP70 expression** in samples exposed to higher levels of pollution.

The reason is that pollution may cause cellular stress and protein damage. Therefore, if the cells are under stress, they are expected to activate protective mechanisms, including increased expression of heat shock proteins.

### Relevance to the Experimental Condition

HSP70 is relevant to pollution exposure because it reflects a general cellular stress response. It does not necessarily indicate exposure to one specific pollutant, but it can show that the cells are experiencing harmful conditions and activating protective pathways.

Therefore, HSP70 is a suitable biomarker for cellular stress caused by pollution exposure.

## Target Gene 2: CYP1A1

### Why I Chose This Gene

I chose **CYP1A1** because it is more directly related to the biological response to chemical pollutants. This gene belongs to the cytochrome P450 family, which includes enzymes involved in the metabolism of foreign compounds.

In contrast to HSP70, which represents a general stress response, CYP1A1 is especially relevant to pollution because it is involved in detoxification pathways and responses to organic contaminants.

### Known Biological Function

CYP1A1 encodes an enzyme that participates in the metabolism of foreign compounds, including certain environmental pollutants. When cells are exposed to compounds such as polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons or persistent organic pollutants, biochemical pathways may be activated that lead to increased CYP1A1 expression.

### Expected Change in Gene Expression

I expect to observe an **increase in CYP1A1 expression** in samples exposed to higher levels of pollution.

If the sperm whale has been exposed to organic pollutants, its cells may increase the expression of genes involved in the metabolism, detoxification, or response to these substances. Therefore, increased CYP1A1 expression may indicate a biological response to pollution exposure.

### Relevance to the Experimental Condition

CYP1A1 is highly relevant to pollution exposure because it can serve as a molecular biomarker of response to contaminants. It does not only indicate that pollutants may be present in the environment, but also that the organism is responding to them biologically.

Combining CYP1A1 with HSP70 allows examination of two different aspects of the pollution response: CYP1A1 reflects detoxification-related activity, while HSP70 reflects general cellular stress.

# Reference Gene

## Reference Gene: YWHAZ

### Why I Chose This Reference Gene

I chose **YWHAZ** as the reference gene because a reference gene should be stably expressed across different samples and experimental conditions. When measuring gene expression using qPCR, the expression of the target genes must be normalized to a gene that is not strongly affected by the treatment or environmental condition.

YWHAZ is a suitable candidate because it is involved in basic cellular processes and is not directly part of the pollution-response, detoxification, or heat-shock pathways examined in this assignment.

### Known Biological Function

YWHAZ encodes a protein from the 14-3-3 protein family, which is involved in general cellular regulation and signaling. Because its function is related to basic cellular activity, it may be suitable for use as a housekeeping gene.

### Why I Expect Its Expression to Remain Stable

I expect YWHAZ expression to remain relatively stable because it is not a direct target gene in the response to pollution, is not directly involved in detoxification, and is not a heat shock protein. Therefore, theoretically, its expression should not change significantly between control samples and pollution-exposed samples.

However, in a real experiment, the stability of the reference gene should be validated before final analysis. If YWHAZ expression changes significantly between experimental groups, another reference gene should be selected, or more than one reference gene should be used.

In this assignment, I chose to examine the possible effect of pollution exposure on gene expression in the sperm whale (*Physeter macrocephalus*).
